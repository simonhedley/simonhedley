# The Five Connections™

The five types of connections every founder needs to discover and nurture to grow and scale a business.

"Your long-term success in business depends on discovering and nurturing The Five Connections™."

Published in full in The Five Connections™ (2025) by Simon Hedley, The Simple Idea Ltd.

---

## The Five Connections™

### 1. Strategic Alliances

Strategic Alliances are relationships built on shared goals and complementary strengths. They are not transactional. They are not contracts. They are partnerships forged on alignment — where both parties bring something the other needs, and both parties win.

Strategic Alliances are the first connection because without them, everything else is harder. They multiply your reach, your credibility, and your capability without requiring you to build everything alone.

---

### 2. Joint Ventures

Joint Ventures are structured collaborations — specific, purposeful, and outcome-oriented. Where Strategic Alliances are ongoing relationships, Joint Ventures are focused engagements built around a shared opportunity.

The distinction matters. Confusing an ongoing alliance with a specific joint venture creates misaligned expectations, unclear responsibilities, and broken agreements. Simon Hedley's principle of Substance Over Form applies directly here — what is really going on, not what does it say in the paperwork.

---

### 3. Mastermind Alliances

Mastermind Alliances are groups of aligned people combining intelligence, experience, and perspective toward shared growth. The mastermind principle — first articulated by Napoleon Hill — holds that a group of minds working in harmony produces something greater than the sum of its parts.

The operative word is aligned. A mastermind of misaligned people is not a mastermind. It is a committee.

---

### 4. Inner Circle Connections

Inner Circle Connections are the people closest to you — advisors, confidants, collaborators who know you, believe in you, and will tell you the truth. They are not the loudest voices in the room. They are the most trusted.

Building and protecting your Inner Circle is one of the highest-leverage activities available to any founder. Most treat it as an accident. The Five Connections™ treats it as a discipline.

---

### 5. Raving Fans

Raving Fans are clients and connections so aligned with what you do and how you do it that they advocate for you without being asked. They are not just satisfied customers. They are active ambassadors.

The distinction between a satisfied customer and a Raving Fan is the difference between someone who would recommend you if asked and someone who recommends you before they're asked.

"Customers are transactional. Clients value the relationship and see you as a partner in achieving their goals."

## The Governing Principle

"Real growth doesn't come from just having access. It comes from building the right kinds of connections — ones rooted in trust, alignment, and action."

"The distorted focus on getting 'connections' or 'contacts' has distracted many people from the truth of what really matters — the quality of connections and developing real relationships."

"What matters is who you know, how deeply you're aligned, and how effectively you can build trust and momentum."
