# The Five Connections™ Colour Codes

A live relationship classification system. Built on traffic light and RAG status principles. Developed with military and special forces veterans, top security advisors, and private equity and banking experts.

From Chapter 8 of The Five Connections™ (2025) by Simon Hedley, The Simple Idea Ltd.

---

## The System

The Five Connections™ Colour Codes assign one of five colours to each person in your ecosystem — to gauge trust, alignment, and collaboration potential.

This is not a personality test. It is not fixed. It is a working tool for making better decisions about where to invest your time, energy, and trust.

"At best, your information is incomplete. At worst, it's inaccurate."

---

## The Five Colours

### 🔴 Red — Stop / Avoid

"People who drain your time, energy, or resources. They may be out of alignment with your values or simply unreliable. You should not share data with them, especially sensitive data."

Red does not mean enemy. It means this relationship costs more than it returns — and requires protection, not investment.

---

### 🟠 Amber — Caution / Potential

"Connections with potential, but who require more due diligence before deeper engagement. You can choose to share data - but carefully and selectively."

Amber is not a verdict. It is a signal to proceed with attention. Many Amber connections become Green. Some become Red. The Colour Code exists precisely to hold that uncertainty honestly.

---

### 🟢 Green — Go / Solid Fit

"People who align with your values and goals. Good, trustworthy connections potentially worth nurturing. You're open to sharing data and potential collaborations."

Green is the foundation of a healthy ecosystem. Not every Green connection becomes Blue or Purple — and that is fine. Green is solid, real, and worth maintaining.

---

### 🔵 Blue — Active Collaboration

"Trusted allies who are actively engaged with you in some capacity."

Blue is where things get built. These are the people you are actively working with — not just aligned with. Trust has been tested. Collaboration is live.

---

### 🟣 Purple — VIP / Inner Circle

"Your most valued, highest-trust connections. These are deep, strategic, long-term connections — often more about who they are than what they have. They receive access, privileges, and treatment worthy of their status."

Purple is not about status or wealth. It is about depth, trust, and alignment over time. Purple connections are rare. They should be treated accordingly.

---

## Rule One

"Assume Nothing and Confirm Everything."

This is the governing principle of the entire system. Every Colour Code assignment is provisional. Every assignment is based on available information. And as the governing principle states — at best, your information is incomplete.

---

## The Movement Principle

"The ultimate goal is to cultivate more Blue and Purple relationships - while reducing time and energy spent on Red and Amber ones."

This is the strategic direction the Colour Codes system is designed to support. Not a fixed snapshot — a moving picture of a healthier ecosystem over time.

---

## What the Colour Codes Are Not

"The Colour Codes aren't set in stone. They are designed to give you a helpful baseline for reflection and conversation."

A Colour Code is not a judgment. It is not permanent. It is a working assessment — honest, current, and subject to change as relationships develop, trust is tested, or new information emerges.

---

## Practical Implementation

The Colour Codes can be recorded in:
- A CRM system
- Project management tools
- Smartphone contacts app

On iPhone, emojis are searchable in the contacts app. The five colour circle emojis — 🔴 🟠 🟢 🔵 🟣 — can be added to any contact name and searched directly. This makes the system live and immediately usable without additional software.

---

## Why Alignment on Colour Codes Matters

Alignment on Colour Code identification — between partners, collaborators, or team members — is cited as a critical factor in whether alliances fly or die.

When two people assign different Colour Codes to the same third party, that discrepancy is not a problem to resolve. It is information. It surfaces misalignment, different data, or different standards — all of which matter before deepening any collaboration.
